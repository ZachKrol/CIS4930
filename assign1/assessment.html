Assessment and Feedback

Question 1

What shape does the paper have to be in before you can begin?

Answer Feedback
Rectangle Incorrect. Many pieces of paper are a rectangle, but you have to modify it before you can begin.
Square Correct. This shape is the basis for almost all origami including this one.
Circle Incorrect. I don't even know how you would start with this shape. Check Step 1 for more information.
Triangle Incorrect. Although this shape is part of the process after some folds, it is not the starting shape.

Question 2

What is a good type of paper material to use?

Answer Feedback
Toilet Paper Incorrect. This is used for wiping your butt and not for origami.
Tissue Paper Incorrect. This paper is too soft and won't be able to retain its shape.
Cardboard Incorrect. This paper is too hard and can't be folded easily.
Printer Paper Correct. Printer paper, origami paper, and loose leaf paper are all good options for paper material.

Question 3

What creases should you initially make to the paper?

Answer Feedback
x crease Incorrect. Although some expert folders like myself can start with this, it's best for beginner's to make all starting creases.
+ crease Incorrect. Check Step 2 for more information.
Both Correct. The more starting creases you have correct means it will be easier to do the other folds later on.
Neither Incorrect. This will lead to imprecise folds throughout the construction.

Question 5

What do you have to do to prepare the opening before blowing air into it?

Answer Feedback
Slightly pinch the edges and pull it open a little bit Correct. This makes inflation easier and smoother.
Spit in the hole Incorrect. Oh boy, this would just lead to wet paper.
Rip the hole open completely Incorrect. This would ruin all of your hard work.
Nothing Incorrect. Check Step 10 for more information.
