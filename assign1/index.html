How to Fold an Origami Balloon

A beginner's guide to folding an origami balloon
that you can blow up and deflate yourself

After following this guide, you will have learned:

  • How to properly select a paper material for making origami
  • The sequence of moves, folds, and tucks to construct a paper balloon
  • Proper hand and mouth placement for optimal inflation

Being able to turn a flat piece of paper into a 3D shape is an amazing skill to have. It is also rewarding seeing your precision and patience pay off in the unique hobby of making origami. I've been folding origami ever since I was a child to pass the time in school, church, or elsewhere. One of my favorite designs to make is a paper balloon that can actually be inflated and retain the air inside of it. Today, I'm going to teach you how to make it step-by-step!

What you will need:

  • A square piece of paper
  • Less than 10 minutes
  • Patience and precision

Step 1

Start with a square piece of paper. Origami paper works perfectly, but if you don't have any origami paper you can start with a rectangular piece of loose leaf paper or printer paper. If you do this, you will need to cut or rip it into a square shape. It is important not to choose a material too soft like tissue paper because you need the final design to hold its shape. Also, a material too hard such as cardboard will make folding and creasing the paper nearly impossible.

Step 2

Make folds in the paper so that there is an 'x' connecting the corners and also a '+' connecting the 4 edges.

Step 3

This is one of the hardest folds of the entire process. Take opposing edges, grab the centers of the edges and slowly bring them underneath the center vertex. The center vertex should start to push up as you bring the edges underneath. At the same time re-crease the diagonals to form a tent shape with the paper so that it can stand up on your table.

Step 4

Take the propped up tent and lay it flat on one of it sides so it looks like a triangle facing you. (There should be 4 individual flaps)

Step 5

Fold one of the flap corners up and connect the endpoint to top of the triangle.

Step 6

Repeat step 5 for all of the flaps. You will do two on one side then flip the paper over and do two on the other side.

Step 7

It should look like a diamond with an opening on the bottom tip. Take the left and right sides of the diamond and fold them to the center-line.

Step 8

Repeat step 7 for all 4 flaps. The top side of the diamond (without the opening) should now have 4 newly loose flap pedals.

Step 9

Take the loose flap from the top and fold it into the crevice that corresponds to it. Repeat for all new pedals.

Step 10

Find the bottom of the diamond with the opening and pull on the edges a little to make the opening slightly bigger. Pinch the edges nearest to the hole, put your lips on the opening, and BLOW!

Step 11

Watch as the paper balloon inflates and enjoy your new piece of art.
